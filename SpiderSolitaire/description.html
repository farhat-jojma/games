How to Play Spider Solitaire

Spider Solitaire is an exciting and free online card game perfect for kids who love puzzles. Your main challenge? Create eight perfect runs of cards from King down to Ace in the same suit and watch them magically disappear! Follow these simple steps to become a Spider Solitaire pro.

Step 1: Set Up the Deck

Ten piles of cards will be shown on your screen. Only the top card in each pile will be face-up, while the rest are hidden until you uncover them.

Step 2: Move Your Cards

Find a card that is exactly one rank lower than another (for example, place a 7 onto an 8). You can stack cards from any suit, but only sequences in the same suit will vanish once completed.

Step 3: Finish a Suit Sequence

Build all the cards from King down to Ace in the same suit. Once a full run is completed, it will disappear, giving you more room to continue playing.

Step 4: Deal Extra Cards

If you run out of moves, press the “Deal” button to place one new card on each pile. Try to leave at least one pile empty before you deal the cards—this helps keep your options open!

Step 5: Win the Game

Keep stacking, clearing, and dealing until all eight suit runs are complete. When every card has been cleared, you’ve won Spider Solitaire!

Tips for Young Champions

  • Always aim to clear an empty pile early.
  • Focus first on forming same-suit groups.
  • Think ahead to plan your moves strategically.

Enjoy every exciting round of Spider Solitaire and improve your card-sorting skills as you play!

Fun Spider Solitaire Tips for Young Players

Build Full Runs: Try to move cards in a way that helps you create a complete sequence from King to Ace. Moving single cards too quickly can block your path, so it's important to plan each step carefully to achieve the perfect order.

Hunt Down Low Cards Early: Aces and Twos are your key helpers. They clear space and open up new moves. Look for these "golden" cards right away and use them to unlock more empty spots on the board.

Peel Back the Piles: Aim to flip at least one hidden card every turn. Each face-down card might reveal a new move or the exact piece you need to complete a sequence.

Shift Whole Sequences: Whenever possible, move entire sequences of cards that are in the same suit rather than moving them one by one. This approach frees up more space for new deals and smarter plays.

Practice Anywhere, Anytime: You don't need to download anything—play Spider Solitaire right in your web browser! Start practicing instantly and watch your skills improve with each game.
